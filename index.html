1. Toggle MIDI Mode (CRITICAL)
Traktor controllers default to NI's private NHL protocol. To use them here, you MUST switch your hardware to standard MIDI Mode:

  • FLX-style Controllers (Direct Boot): Remove the power plug, hold down the FLX button, and connect the USB/power to the computer.
  • Kontrol S3 (Hold): Press SHIFT + EXT (Deck Select) until buttons flash.
  • Other Controllers: S2/S4 hold SHIFT + BROWSE or PLAY.

2. Traktor Kontrol S3 Premium 4-Deck Mapping
The 4 fader decks on the S3 are auto-mapped to individual instruments in C-A-B-D physical layout (left-to-right):

DECK C (Leftmost) → KICK (Ch 12)
  • Fader (CC 1): Kick Instrument Volume
  • Filter Knob (CC 4): Instrument Step Intensity (1–16)
  • Low EQ (CC 5): Kick Pitch Tune (Default: 1.0x)
  • Mid EQ (CC 6): Kick Envelope Decay (Default: 1.0x)
  • High EQ (CC 7): Click Transient Punch (Default: 1.0x)
  • Gain Knob (CC 8): Absolute Distortion Drive (Bi-directional)
  • Buttons: ON → Mute, CUE → Solo
DECK A (Left Center) → DRUMS (Ch 10)
  • Fader (CC 1): Drums Instrument Volume
  • Filter Knob (CC 4): Instrument Step Intensity (1–16)
  • Low EQ (CC 5): Perc Envelope Decay Length
  • Mid EQ (CC 6): Hat Tone Cutoff Sweep
  • High EQ (CC 7): Snappy Noise Level
  • Gain Knob (CC 8): Absolute Distortion Drive (Bi-directional)
  • Buttons: ON → Mute, CUE → Solo
DECK B (Right Center) → SYNTH (Ch 11)
  • Fader (CC 1): Synth Instrument Volume
  • Filter Knob (CC 4): Instrument Step Intensity (1–16)
  • Low EQ (CC 5): Synth Filter Cutoff (LPF)
  • Mid EQ (CC 6): Resonant Peak Modifier
  • High EQ (CC 7): Synth Envelope Decay Time
  • Gain Knob (CC 8): Delay FX Feedback Send (Bi-directional)
  • Buttons: ON → Mute, CUE → Solo
DECK D (Rightmost) → BASS (Ch 13)
  • Fader (CC 1): Bass Instrument Volume
  • Filter Knob (CC 4): Instrument Step Intensity (1–16)
  • Low EQ (CC 5): Bass Filter Cutoff (LPF)
  • Mid EQ (CC 6): Resonant Filter Sharpness
  • High EQ (CC 7): Bass Sub Level Boost
  • Gain Knob (CC 8): Absolute Distortion Drive (Bi-directional)
  • Buttons: ON → Mute, CUE → Solo

3. Global Hardware Assignments
Mixer center console faders and master controls are mapped directly to global engine params:

  • Play Buttons (Ch 2/4/6/8, C#-1): Pressing any physical play button triggers Play/Pause globally.
  • Crossfader (Ch 14, CC 1): Fully Left (0) = Kick + Drums only; Center (64) = All; Fully Right (127) = Synth + Bass only. Uses high-fidelity constant-power curves.
  • Master Vol Knob (Ch 14, CC 12): Controls global master volume.
  • Booth Vol Knob (Ch 14, CC 4): Synth Morph Macro Control Slider (0–100%).
  • Tempo Sliders (Ch 2/4/6/8, CC 42): Pitch faders execute high-precision relative BPM adjustment (+/- 50%) based on active preset's base speed. Supports physical deck switching seamlessly.

4. Live Monitor Verification
Move any control on your hardware. If you see activity in the Live MIDI Input Monitor above, your controller is successfully sending MIDI! Read the logged Channel & CC numbers to check your mappings.
